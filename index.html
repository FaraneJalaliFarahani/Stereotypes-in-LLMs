Before you begin — please read carefully

A semantic axis can be defined by a pair of antonymous adjectives, e.g., poor vs. rich. Position of a social group (e.g., "CEOs", "homeless people") on that semantic axis serves as a measure of stereotype association.

For example, CEOs are expected to be associated closer to the rich pole, while homeless people are associated closer to the poor pole.

Examples
Stereotypical axes
  • unfriendly ↔ friendly — warmth; social groups differ in perceived warmth
  • immoral ↔ moral — morality; moral character varies by different social group
Non-stereotypical axes
  • abaxial ↔ adaxial — plant anatomy; no social group mapping
  • basiscopic ↔ acroscopic — purely biological; no social group mapping
Your task

For each antonym pair, consider the semantic axis it constructs. You will see the negative pole on the left and the positive pole on the right. Answer two questions in order:

  1. Q1 Is this semantic axis related to humans? — Does it primarily describe traits, behaviours, or categories that apply to humans? This is a prerequisite of a semantic axes to be cosnidered as stereotypical semantic axes.
  2. Q2 Is this semantic axis stereotypical? — From your perspective, are different social groups associated with different positions along this axis? Would you expect certain groups to be closer to one pole than the other?

Use Unsure when you genuinely cannot decide. Optional notes are available for any pair.
