Before you begin — please read carefully

In this annotation task, you are given two antonyms. We want you to write down the groups of people that come to mind when you think about each word.

For example, for the antonyms poor and rich, you might write "homeless people" under poor and "CEOs" under rich. This reflects a stereotype that exists about those groups. There are no right or wrong answers — we are interested in your genuine, spontaneous associations.

Examples
Groups come to mind
  • unfriendly ↔ friendly — e.g. "politicians" vs "nurses"
  • immoral ↔ moral — e.g. "criminals" vs "priests"
No groups come to mind
  • increased ↔ decreased — a quantity, not about people
  • concave ↔ convex — geometry, no people involved
What to do

For each adjective you will see a text box — write any groups of people that spontaneously come to mind for that word. Separate multiple groups with commas. Leave it empty if nothing comes to mind.

You will also see a toggle: Applicable to humans / Not applicable. If the words describe a purely physical, chemical, or biological phenomenon with no relevance to people, mark it Not applicable.

⚠ Important: Please complete the annotation in one sitting without interruption. Taking breaks may shift your perspective and affect consistency. Progress is not saved if the browser tab is closed — export before closing.
