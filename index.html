Instructions

In this task, you will be shown an antonym pair (two opposite adjectives) and a set of words. For each word, rate where it falls on the scale between the two opposites.

Example

Antonym pair: poor ↔ rich

Rate "students": are students rather poor or rather rich?

1 (poor) — 2 — 3 (neither) — 4 — 5 (rich)

Rate "tables": the concept of poor/rich does not apply to tables → mark Not applicable.

Some words are social groups (e.g., "nurses", "CEOs") and some are general nouns (e.g., "tables", "curiosity"). Rate all of them the same way — based on your intuitive associations.

For a small number of axes, you will also be asked to suggest a word that you would rate at each extreme of the scale. This is optional but very helpful.

⏱ Tip: Go with your gut feeling — first impressions are best. Don't overthink. The task is designed to be quick: roughly 1–2 minutes per axis.
