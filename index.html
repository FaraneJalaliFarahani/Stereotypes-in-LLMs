Before you begin — please read carefully

In this annotation task, you are given an antonym pair (two opposite adjectives). We want you to write down the groups of people that come to mind when you think about each adjective. For example, for the antonym pair poor ↔ rich, you might write "homeless people" under poor and "CEOs" under rich. This reflects a stereotype you have about those groups of people in your mind.

Examples
Stereotypical antonyms
  • unfriendly ↔ friendly — e.g. "politicians" vs "nurses"
  • immoral ↔ moral — e.g. "criminals" vs "priests"
Non-stereotypical antonyms
  • increased ↔ decreased — a quantity, not about people
  • concave ↔ convex — geometry, no people involved
What to do

For each adjective, you will see a text box — write any groups of people that spontaneously come to mind for that adjective. Separate multiple groups with commas. Leave it empty if nothing comes to mind.

You will also see a toggle: Applicable to humans / Not applicable. If the adjective describes something not relevant to people, such as a purely physical, chemical, or biological phenomenon, mark it Not applicable.

If you mark a pair as applicable, you will be asked to rate how stereotypical the associations are on a scale from 1 (very weak) to 5 (very strong).

⚠ Important: Please complete the annotation in one sitting without interruption. Taking breaks may shift your perspective and affect consistency. Progress is not saved if the browser tab is closed — export before closing.
